## Do you recognise this in your daily work?

1. Spending significant time on manual tasks, such as checking files and looking for information, is not only time-inefficient but also more prone to human error.
2. Collecting data from suppliers is a lengthy process, requiring constant email exchanges to receive all documents, which might even be unreadable.
3. Having incomplete or inaccurate information makes it difficult to maintain consistent quality, compliance with industry standards, and conformity to customer specifications.
4. Not being able to visualize data. [Visualization tools](/blog/how-box-plots-and-violin-plots-can-be-used-to-ensure-material-quality/) are essential for gaining insight into quality trends and identifying potential issues.

## The impact of inefficient quality management

Now you might ask yourself: how does this impact me? The costs of inefficient quality management in the steel industry are multiple. Some of the most important consequences include:

- Safety risks: In oil and gas, safety is paramount. Inconsistent quality management practices can pose serious safety risks, leading to accidents or equipment failures.
- Inconsistent product quality: Inconsistent quality management practices lead to variations in product quality, affecting reliability and safety.
- Human errors: Manual processes are prone to human error, which can lead to rework or even safety incidents.
- Lack of traceability: When there is no clear trail of how and where an issue originated, resolving it becomes challenging and time-consuming.
- Transparency issues: Without clear visibility into the supply chain, it is hard to maintain compliance with industry standards and customer requirements.
- Compliance issues: Meeting customer and industry standards is fundamental. Inefficient quality management can lead to non-compliances.
- Customer complaints and loss of customers: Ultimately, poor quality leads to dissatisfied customers.

## Making quality management more efficient

To overcome the challenges of inefficient quality management, companies can implement different tools. Here are a few ways in which you can improve your quality management system:

1. Automate non-conformity detection: Automated systems can identify and flag non-conformities in real time, allowing you to tackle the issue immediately.
2. [Standardize across manufacturers](/blog/the-importance-of-data-standardization/): Implementing standard quality processes across your supply chain ensures consistency.
3. Automate graph generation: Visualization tools can help quality managers identify trends across data.
4. Track certification status in real time: With real-time data monitoring, you can instantly verify the certification status of materials and products, ensuring compliance and reducing risks.
5. Use centralized dashboards: A centralized quality management dashboard provides a comprehensive view of the entire supply chain, showing at a glance what matters most.
